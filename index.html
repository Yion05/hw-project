About WenCal MindArc Studios

‚≠ê

WenCal MindArc Studios, founded by WONG HUEI WEN, is developing 'Island of Light' a creative emotional adventure game to help children, teenagers, young adults facing stress, anxiety and emotional burnout.

We provide a safe, non-judgmental space for self-expression and healing through a unique 'art-psychology' system where players literally draw their world into existence. Our AI-powered backend can even visualize emotions and securely connect players with professional therapists.

"Our mission is to build games that touch hearts, heal minds and illuminate the path to self-discovery through the universal language of art and color."

- Wong Huei Wen, Founder & Creative Director

Our Product Strategy Canvas

A strategic deep-dive into the 11 core elements defining our product, its value, and its path to market.

1. Problem Statement

Core Question

What top problems are we solving for our customers?

Relevance

Frames feature priorities and validates whether the product addresses real pain points.

Key Product Implication

Problem: Teenagers & young adults face high stress, anxiety, and lack a safe space for self-expression.

Implication: The product must be non-competitive, gentle, and private-first. "Winning" is defined as "expressing."

2. Customer Segments

Core Question

Who are we creating value for?

Relevance

Defines target users, personas, and use cases. Guides UX/UI design and testing.

Key Product Implication

  • Target 1 (Core): Teens/Young Adults (15-35) seeking mindfulness and stress relief.
  • Target 2 (Wellness): Educators & counselors needing mental wellness tools.

3. Unfair Advantage

Core Question

What unique asset or capability can't be easily copied?

Relevance

Influences defensive product design and long-term differentiation.

Our "Secret Sauce"

  • Your unique, healing art style.
  • The proprietary "Art-Psychology" system.
  • The AI-powered player-drawn shop generation.
  • The integrated, secure network of professional therapists.

4. Value Proposition

Core Question

What value are we delivering?

Relevance

Shapes core features and benefits. Determines MVP scope.

Key Product Implication

Core Value: A safe, beautiful, emotional space for healing through art and play.

Premium Value: Direct, secure access to professional psychological help, turning gameplay into tangible wellness.

5. Channels

Core Question

How do we reach and deliver value to customers?

Relevance

Influences product architecture and integration needs (e.g., app store, web).

Key Product Implication

  • Digital: App Store, Google Play, Steam.
  • Marketing: TikTok/YouTube (emotional shorts, art creation).
  • Professional: Partnerships with mental health NGOs and universities.

6. Customer Relationships

Core Question

What type of relationship do customers expect?

Relevance

Impacts design of user support, onboarding flows, and retention mechanisms.

Key Product Implication

  • In-Game: A supportive, creative, non-toxic community.
  • App-Level: Highly personalized experiences (as per your feedback), such as personalized email summaries of emotional insights, or notifications at the end of transactions ("Your creation has inspired 3 other players!").

7. Revenue Streams

Core Question

How does the business earn money?

Relevance

Guides pricing logic, feature tiering, and product roadmap prioritization.

Key Product Implication

  • Freemium: F2P with optional cosmetics (outfits, brushes).
  • Subscription (Key): "Premium Wellness" for therapist access & reports.
  • B2B: "Studio Edition" licenses for schools/clinics.

8. Key Resources

Core Question

What assets are critical to deliver value?

Relevance

Determines tech stack, infrastructure, and team skills required.

Key Product Implication

  • Talent: Your art & creative direction.
  • Tech: Game engine (Unity/Unreal), AI generation models, secure server backend (HIPAA-compliant).
  • Partners: A vetted network of licensed psychologists.

9. Key Activities

Core Question

What must the company do to deliver its value proposition?

Relevance

Translates into development sprints, feature milestones, and testing priorities.

Key Product Implication

  • Emotional game design.
  • Building & training the "draw-a-shop" AI generator.
  • Building the secure therapist connection backend.
  • Using AI tools to build our own website, logo, and marketing pics.

10. Key Partners

Core Question

Who helps us deliver value or reduce risk?

Relevance

Shapes build-vs-buy decisions and integration planning with external vendors.

Key Product Implication

  • CRITICAL: Licensed Psychologists & Therapists (for consultation & as service providers).
  • Mental health NGOs (for guidance & reach).
  • App Stores (Apple, Google, Steam).

11. Cost Structure

Core Question

What are the major costs and resource needs?

Relevance

Defines budget allocation for product design, prototyping, and scaling.

Key Product Implication

  • Must-Spend: Server costs, therapist network management/payment, core art/sound.
  • Can-Save: Using AI for website/logo creation, community-driven marketing. (As per your "Financial Strategy").
